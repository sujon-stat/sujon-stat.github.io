About

I'm a final-year undergraduate in the Department of Statistics, Jagannath University (16th batch), based in Dhaka, Bangladesh. My work sits at the intersection of statistical method and public health practice — I analyze large-scale population health survey data and build tools that make rigorous analysis more accessible.

As a researcher at StatAid Research Lab, I work with WHO STEPS, DHS, and MICS datasets to measure non-communicable disease multimorbidity, quantify health inequalities, and estimate the concentration index. I'm currently developing an R package (SurveyNCD) that packages these workflows into a reusable, CRAN-bound toolkit.

My research interests center on explainable machine learning in healthcare, survival analysis, multilevel modeling, and maternal & child health inequalities. I'm also interested in epidemic surveillance systems — combining classical epidemiological models with modern forecasting techniques.

I'm building toward a funded master's or PhD in biostatistics, epidemiology, or public health data science. Target programs include Harvard Chan, Johns Hopkins BSPH, LSHTM, Karolinska, and the Universities of Melbourne and Adelaide. I'm currently preparing for IELTS and GRE.

EpiAgent

Epidemic Surveillance Pipeline

A Python-based epidemic surveillance pipeline combining SEIR modeling, Bayesian Rt estimation (Cori et al. method), BOCPD changepoint detection, XGBoost+SHAP forecasting, and automated dashboards. Submitted to the Google/Kaggle 5-Day AI Agents Intensive capstone competition. Strengths: clean architectural separation of LLM reasoning from deterministic computation, strong test coverage.

KAP Research (Skincare)

A full Knowledge, Attitudes, and Practices study on skincare behaviors, with a reproducible R analysis pipeline that generates all manuscript tables automatically. A research report on the prognostic value of Red Cell Distribution Width (RDW) in children with Community-Acquired Pneumonia (CAP). Examines RDW as a readily available laboratory marker for predicting disease severity and clinical outcomes in pediatric populations.
